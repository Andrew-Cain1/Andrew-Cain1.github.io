Brief  Biography

 

Paquita  Sabrafen

 

 

I

nternationally renowned artist, Paquita Sabrafen, was born in 1931 in Barcelona, Spain.  She has dedicated her life to her work and is a master in her profession.  Having specialized in portraits, her brush strokes convey the spirit of her subject onto the canvas.  A combination of control over her instruments and colours, with the powerfulness of a creative spirit, enables her to convey many sentiments and ideals in her paintings.

 

Sabrafen’s paintings are in the collections of numerous prestigious National Art Museums worldwide including; The Hermitage Museum (St Petersburg, Russia);The National Museum of Czechoslovakia in Prague; Nagasaki Art Museum, Japan; The Provincial Museum of Albacete, Castilla La Mancha, Spain; Art Gallery of NSW, Sydney, Australia; The National Art Museum of China (2005). Appreciation of Sabrafen’s work has been expressed by the Directors and Senior Curators of these Art Museums.

 

Sabrafen’s oil painting ‘Woman Looking at Herself in a Lake’ was selected amongst thousands of entrants for the Nika exhibition and  occupied a prime place in the Tokyo Metropolitan Art Museum.

Other oils are in the Atomic Bomb Museum; the 26 Martyrs Museum; Nagasaki Town Hall. Also they may be found in many churches and embassies. Some on major altars, such as the hermitage of Capuchins in Alicante, ‘Nuestra Señora de Orito’, a very large oil painting, ‘San Pascual’ where approximately 200,000 people pilgrim each year and it is said that Sabrafen’s paintings have inspired much devotion.  Others are in the church and convent of the Carmelites in the pueblo of Lietor (Albacete, Castilla la Mancha), wherein there are many works painted by the artist and others which she has restored.  The painting of “Cruz Ortiz Real” the Nun declared a Saint by the Pope on the 21st of March, 2004 hangs in the founding church of the Salessianas in Alicante, Spain. Life size posters of this painting were carried on banners to the Vatican and hang in the churches and convents of the Salessianas all over the world.

Paquita had a tumultuous childhood, beginning with the death of her mother at age 5 and moving from her home in Barcelona to France due to the Spanish Civil War.  It then, culminated in her having to move again due to the Second World War to Buenos Aires, Argentina.  It is here that she began her journey in Art, taking lessons from famed Artist Andrea Morch and other celebrated Artists such as Vincent Puig, Moises, Guinart, Huget and Viladrich.

Since 1950, the first solo exhibitions of her oils were held in Argentina. She exhibited on various occasions at ‘Galeria Muller’, in the Alvear Palace in Buenos Aires, and the ‘Galeria Renon’ in Rosario.  She became the preferred portrait painter of distinguished society in Buenos Aires.

She has combined portrait and figure painting with landscapes and still life, and is a Master in her profession.  Among her latest works are the painting titled “Torn by War” – Eric Campbell, ABC Foreign Correspondent.  This painting expresses his personal tragedy, the loss of his cameraman, Paul Moran who was the first Australian to die in the Iraq War.

 Another recent work is the portrait of Masako Ide, who in October 2004, was personally awarded a medal from the Emperor of Japan for her service to her country in the area of public health.  The painting was completed March, 2005.  These are historic paintings recording important incidents.  She has also painted members of the Fijian Royal Family and noted politicians.

Sabrafen has received official honours in a number of countries, both for her art and her other qualities.  Although she considers being a mother to be her greatest work.  It is fascinating to see how she maintains her vocation with unstinting energy, thanks to her talent and her love of life which enables her to show in her works the creative aspect of human endeavour.

Over the last 15 years she has divided her time between Australia and Spain.  She has travelled extensively throughout the world. In a pueblo of La Mancha she composes large works in an atmosphere of peace and healthy abundance.

In La Mancha she lives in an historic 300 year old mansion, which she has restored for her work and comfort.  The Villa – Studio is situated on a mountainside, looking out over other mountains and a river flowing beneath.  Coaches filled with Tourists have requested entry into the house as it is filled with her works and is itself like a Gallery/ Museum.

Her work and the fruits it bears are permanent.
